Most "company brain" products are evaluated on retrieval quality.
That's a search benchmark.

A company brain is the substrate for reliable AI automation, not just AI retrieval. The right benchmark measures four things search benchmarks miss.

The four dimensions

01 Freshness

When the policy changed yesterday, how long until the brain reflects it? Measured in hours, not weeks.

Why: Stale knowledge is the most expensive failure mode in production — the agent confidently doing the wrong thing.

02 Audience-safety

Can the brain enforce that operator-only knowledge cannot ever load into a customer-facing agent session?

Why: Required for regulated industries, managed-services operators, and any business with material asymmetry between operator and customer knowledge.

03 Portability

Can the same brain output run on Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, OpenAI Agents SDK, Lindy, Devin? Or is it locked to one runtime?

Why: Vendor lock-in is the buyer's #1 fear in 2026. Skill-file artifacts are the answer.

04 Operational provenance

When the agent cites a fact, can it point to the email thread, Slack channel, or meeting that produced it?

Why: Required to debug hallucinations, re-validate sources, and pass an audit.

Each dimension scored 0–5 with concrete criteria. Full rubric: DIMENSIONS.md.

What we deliberately don't score on

  • Raw retrieval quality. Covered by RAG benchmarks elsewhere.
  • Distribution to F500. A popularity contest, not an evaluation. BizBrain OS would lose.
  • Ecosystem maturity. Glean, Microsoft, Notion all win. Different question.
  • Pricing. Listed in the per-product files where public; not used for scoring.

If you need to find the document where the policy is written, use Glean. If you need an agent to do the work the way your specific company does it, that's what this benchmark measures.

FAQ

You scored your own product. Of course it wins.

We scored BizBrain OS on the dimensions we built it for. We deliberately did NOT score on dimensions where we'd lose — raw enterprise-search recall, distribution to F500, partner ecosystem maturity. Those matter and we're behind.

Pick the right tool. If you need to find the document where the policy is written, use Glean. If you need an agent to do the work the way your company does it, this benchmark measures the dimensions that matter.

How were competitors scored?

From their public engineering blogs, public docs, public pricing pages, and where applicable, hands-on testing. We do not score from secondary reporting. Where evidence is ambiguous, we mark [UNCERTAIN] in the per-product file and explain the gap.

Is this a marketing page?

Yes and no. It's a marketing page that earns the right to be one by publishing the methodology openly, scoring honestly, and accepting public contestation of every score.

Disagree with a score? File an issue. We review weekly.

Why these four dimensions and not others?

They're the dimensions that distinguish a company-brain product from an enterprise-search product. They're what regulated buyers ask about within the first 30 minutes of a serious evaluation. They're not exhaustive — they're a deliberate frame.

Will you add my product?

Open an issue with the product name and a 1-paragraph architectural summary. We add new products quarterly. We score products that publicly position themselves as "company brain," "knowledge platform," or "AI knowledge layer." We don't score every memory library, vector DB, or workflow framework.

What this benchmark is for

Buyers: ask your AI vendors which dimensions they score on, and where. The vendor who can answer freshness/audience/portability/provenance with specifics is in a different category than the vendor who answers with adjectives.

Builders: if you're building in this space, the rubric tells you what serious buyers will ask in 2026. Build for those dimensions or be ready to lose evaluations to competitors who do.

Investors: this is the category Tom Blomfield called for in YC's Summer 2026 RFS. The differentiation is not "another company brain idea." It's a working, governed compiler between messy operational reality and reliable agent action.
